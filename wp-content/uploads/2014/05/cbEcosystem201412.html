For its ninth Nobel, Bell Labs counts on Israeli tech


Marcus Weldon, the current president of one of the most celebrated technology centers in the world, has high hopes for his local team.

Bell Labs on Monday night inaugurated its Israeli branch – the group’s first out-of-the-US location – at the Kfar Saba offices of Alcatel-Lucent, the multinational communications firm that now owns the organization. The Israel lab actually began work several months ago, but got its official kick-off when Weldon, along with other top Bell Labs officials and alumni, gave the Israel site their official stamp of approval.
