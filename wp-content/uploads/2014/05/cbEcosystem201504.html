NFV MASHUP SERIES
Scaling of Diameter Peers in NFV environments using virtual Diameter Signaling Controller (vDSC) with Tieto®

To fully embrace the concepts of Network Function Virtualization (NFV) Service providers will look for deploying highly scalable and elastic vIMS and vEPC networks with high availability and built in dynamics to manage network resources in a controlled manner. To successfully scale up and down these type of virtual network functions (VNFs),, managing and load balancing of the associated Diameter control signaling traffic is a must.

In this use case, Alcatel-Lucent, with CloudBand™ Ecosystem Program member—Tieto®—have worked together to demonstrate how to onboard their VNF and load balance Diameter control traffic. By exploiting the concept of VNF service chaining and in combination with the load balancing capabilities of Tieto’s scalable virtual Diameter Signaling Controller (vDSC), remote peer nodes (e.g. PCRF and HSS VNFs) are allowed to scale, seamlessly taken in and out of service, without any need for reconfiguration at originating peer nodes.

This webinar will present how carriers can manage Diameter signaling traffic between Diameter peers in a dynamic NFV environment while also getting essential Key Performance Input data for network monitoring of Diameter traffic.

IT HAPPENED
MPLS SDN World Congress together with the NFV & SDN Summit in Paris with more than 1400 attendees was an excellent event for service providers and vendors to meet and discuss the newest trends. This year there was a much larger focus on NFV than in the past. Alcatel-Lucent began the event with a technical workshop examining the network evolution driving success in the cloud era. In the following days, numerous customers visited the Alcatel-Lucent booth featuring two demo stations: The first one showed a smaller scale, live version of the Lean Operations demonstration and the second showed network & service management of virtual network services as well as dynamic WAN service delivery and optimization with the Network Service Platform. Both demonstrations triggered intense discussions about the evolution of networking – “This is clearly the target solution for NFV” said one customer.
