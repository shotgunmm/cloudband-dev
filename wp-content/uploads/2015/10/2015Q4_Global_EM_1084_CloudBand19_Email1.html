Appledore Scorecard - Alcatel-Lucent a leading supplier in the NFV MANO marketplace
Appledore Research Group recently completed research on leading suppliers in the market of Management and Orchestration solutions. Appledore evaluated 12 suppliers against a set of 10 criteria for operational support of NFV, SDN, and Cloud services. Moving NFV into commercial networks : the network functions vendors perspective
The adoption of Virtualization by the Network Functions supplier is a determining success factor for NFV. Four suppliers of network applications recently shared with TelecomTV their view on the progress of virtualization and the opportunities and challenges of deploying NFV based solution in commercial networks. You can watch a short highlight of interviews with F5, Radware, Procera and JDSU here.

Enabling the industry shift from hardware towards software
In this short interview, Idan Mor, Sr Director CloudBand Alliances looks back at the genesis of CloudBand, an initiative that started out from the motivation of Alcatel-Lucent for moving its own software from dedicated hardware-based solution towards more flexible cloud based solutions and that is today enabling the larger industry in its “Cloudshift” transformation journey.
 
BLOG
 
LightReading - Bringing Together NFV MANO & OSS :
In this 3rd post of her Blog Series on NFV, Caroline Chappell looks at the complementary role of NFV Management and Network Orchestration with the Operation and Support System and the need for the OSS to evolve to accommodate the highly dynamic environment created by NFV. She presents a vision for a realistic gradual evolution of OSS that could hold back the transformation to NFV. Continue reading Caroline´s blog, and access the previous two parts here and here.

Tom Nolle - Could ALU’s Recent “NFV Wins” Show a Shift in Focus for the Industry? :
In this blog post, Tom Nolle, President of CIMI Corp., shares his thought about what the recent NFV wins of Alcatel-Lucent in China and with Telefonica reveal about how the industry is embracing NFV. Tom argues that these successes validate the vision of Cloud-centric future, with operations efficiency and service agility as the prime benefits of NFV. Read more here.

Transformation for NFV - fifth lesson: professional services in NFV – where do they fit ?
A central promise of NFV is to optimize operational expenditure for telecom projects. This necessarily entails a has a huge impact on professional services, which traditionally have occupied a significant, and sometimes uncontrollable part of these projects. But rather than spelling the end of professional services, the transformation for NFV is an opportunity for vendors, integrators and service provider to focus on high added-value (design, architecture etc…) and strategic activities rather than on low end (installation, deployment …) activities. Guy examines more in detail this transformation and the opportunities that it offers in his latest blog post.

The need for holistic approach to mobile device security:
The latest Alcatel-Lucent security report shows the trend of mobile malware infections increasing in number, sophistication and, most importantly, severity. At the same time, this trend represents an opportunity for service providers to provide a network based security solutions, such as Alcatel-Lucent Motive® Security Guardian which can leverage a cloud-based deployment architecture to rapidly deploy at a network-wide level responses to new security threats.
