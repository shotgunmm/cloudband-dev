Dear Ecosystem Members and Friends,

We are constantly developing and improving the capabilities of this demo and so please, come and visit us at the Carrier Network Virtualization conference in Palo Alto, California starting Nov 30. Together, let's continue the good work and get even more POCs out of the labs!

Valerie.
NEWS
 

CloudBand 3.0 Launch (June 2015):
This year's biggest news was the launch in June of CloudBand 3.0, the world's first carrier-grade, production ready, OpenStack based NFV Platform. This major milestone brings enhancements and optimizations for production deployments and reduces the NFV entry threshold for first deployments. In particular, all critical component of CloudBand including OpenStack are now fully redundant. With this major new release CloudBand Nodes can be upgraded without downtime, a dramatic improvement for management and operations of these networks.

For more information, read the CloudBand 3.0 Solution Sheet or watch again Ron Haberman, General Manager for our CloudBand business unit, talking about CloudBand 3.0.

Cloud communication and collaboration with Rapport on CloudBand
Alcatel-Lucent Rapport is a cloud-optimized communication and collaboration software platform for service providers and large enterprises. The introduction of VoLTE, VoWi-Fi and advanced communication services for enterprises are compelling events for adopting the new virtualized Rapport™ software on CloudBand™. However the way VNFs, like Rapport, are managed and orchestrated by the NFV Platform in a cloud environment is a critical part of a successful NFV roll-out. You can learn more about the architecture of this integration and the associated benefits in this application notes.

Alcatel-Lucent: a year of virtualization of Network Functions
A major goal of NFV is giving the network functions the freedom to move from dedicated, often purpose built hardware into a software function running in a Cloud environment and managed and orchestrated by an NFV platform. To achieve this goal requires some careful rearchitecting of the function to create truly cloud ready VNFs. In Alcatel-Lucent, not only are we supplying the CloudBand NFV platform over which third party network functions can run, we are also leading a comprehensive internal effort in virtualizing our own solutions. Some major achievements in this is area in 2015 include:

  • The virtualized Residential Gateway (vRGW)
  • The virtualized Content Delivery Network ( vCDN)
  • Rapport Communication solutions suite, built upon the Virtualized IMS (vIMS),  the founding block for delivering VoLTE
  • The virtualized Packet Core
  • The virtualized Service Router : The flagship Aclatel-Lucent 7750-SR edge router running on an off-the-shelf COTS x86 server.
  • The virtualized Radio Access Network ( vRAN)

  WEBINARS & VIDEO
 
Applying Lean operational principles to NFV : 
To maximize the opportunity presented by SDN and NFV we need to start planning operational tasks from a new perspective.  Lean principles can be applied to the implementation and operation of NFV to enable efficient and automated dynamic services. Alcatel-Lucent’s Lean NFV Ops program focuses on architecting the effective delivery of these dynamic services by operating with highly efficient solutions at any scale.  This Demonstration outlines the Alcatel-Lucent lean NFV Ops framework and illustrates how Lean NFV Ops can be used to launch VPN and VoLTE services using the Cloudband platform.

Lean NFV operations demonstration:
The Alcatel-Lucent lean NFV Ops Demonstration is an interactive showcase of a fully virtualized end-to-end solution for operations management, which is running over a hundred virtual machines. This is a high availability (HA) system subject to a comprehensive set of lifecycle automation. This live demonstration presents a virtualization catalog structured around an application store and launchpad coupled with rapid service instantiation.  A holistic approach to life cycle management (LCM) and Reliability, Availability  and Serviceability ( RAS) tests is demonstrated along with advanced Service Function Chainings (SFC). Watch the demonstration here. You can also view a presentation,  or  read the 2 pages brief.

Achieving Lean NFV Operations:
These three parts Lightreading webinar series show how Lean NFV operations use automation, virtualization and integration to build dynamic services. The webinar series demonstrate how NFV can help preserve quality of experience and service continuity during management operations.  Each webinar cover one key aspect of the transformation into a true NFV enabled service provider:  Building the right open NFV platform ,  Architecting and enabling automatic VNF deployment and Transforming the OSS for physical and virtual working.

Dynamic Service Orchestration across the Network and NFV for Improved Customer Experience: 
There is a real opportunity for service providers to simplify their OSS by introducing a highly automated and dynamic approach to service operations. Closing the loop between the Network and IT operations systems that are supporting service fulfillment and services assurance processes is key to delivering the OSS simplification and resulting agility capabilities for the introduction of NFV and SDN.  Learn about how the Motive Dynamic Operations (OSS) portfolio will help service providers bridge the gap between next generation networks and customer experience through abstraction, automation, and analytics and provide end-to-end service orchestration that delivers full management of end-customer and network services across multi-domain VNFs & PNFs.  Register for the webinar  on October 28th, 3 PM GMT here.
